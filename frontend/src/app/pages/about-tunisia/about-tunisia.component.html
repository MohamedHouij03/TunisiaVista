Geography

A Land of Extraordinary Contrast

Tunisia occupies a strategic position on the North African Mediterranean coast, stretching from the green Kroumirie mountains in the northwest to the Grand Erg Oriental's sand sea in the south. Despite its modest size (163,610 km²), Tunisia packs an astonishing range of landscapes.

The country's geography divides naturally into distinct zones: fertile Tell region in the north, the Sahel coastal plains, the high Dorsal mountain chain, and the vast pre-Saharan and Saharan regions of the south. Over 1,300km of coastline frames the eastern edge, offering beaches that range from Mediterranean crystal clarity to warm Gulf of Gabes shallows.
